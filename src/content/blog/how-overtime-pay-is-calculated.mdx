Overtime pay is additional pay for hours worked beyond a threshold (often 40 hours/week) for eligible workers.

The most common overtime rate is **time-and-a-half**:

**Overtime Rate = Regular Hourly Rate × 1.5**

To estimate overtime pay for a week or pay period, use the [Overtime Pay Calculator](/overtime-calculator).

## The basic overtime calculation

If you work 46 hours in a week:

- Regular hours: 40
- Overtime hours: 6

If your hourly rate is $25:

- Overtime rate: $25 × 1.5 = $37.50
- Overtime pay: 6 × $37.50 = $225

## Double time and other multipliers

Some employers or jurisdictions pay:

- **double time (2.0×)** after a higher threshold
- different multipliers for holidays or special shifts

## Overtime vs salary: who is eligible?

Eligibility depends on role classification (exempt vs non-exempt), job duties, and wage rules. Many salaried roles are exempt, but not all.

## FAQ

### Is overtime always time-and-a-half?
Often, but not always. Some workplaces pay different multipliers, and certain roles have different rules. Use 1.5× as a standard planning default.

### Do I pay more taxes on overtime?
Overtime increases your income, so you may see higher withholding, but the tax you owe depends on total annual income. Model your full-year estimate with the [After-Tax Income Calculator](/after-tax-income-calculator).

### Why did my paycheck withholding jump on an overtime check?
Payroll withholding formulas can treat a bigger check as if you earn that amount every pay period, which can temporarily increase withholding.

### Does overtime change my hourly rate?
It changes your overtime rate for overtime hours. Your base hourly rate remains the same.
