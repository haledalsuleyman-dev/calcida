## The Zero-Tax Club

As of 2024, nine states do not levy a state income tax on earned wages:

1.  Alaska
2.  Florida
3.  Nevada
4.  New Hampshire
5.  South Dakota
6.  Tennessee
7.  Texas
8.  Washington
9.  Wyoming

*(Note: NH taxes investment income, and WA taxes capital gains for high earners). Estimate your potential liability with our [Capital Gains Tax Calculator](/capital-gains-tax-calculator).*

## The Paycheck Impact

If you earn **$100,000** a year:
*   **In California (9.3% max rate):** You might pay ~$6,000 in state tax.
*   **In Texas:** You pay **$0**.

That is an extra $500 a month in your pocket just for crossing a state line.

## The Catch: There Is No Free Lunch

States need revenue for roads, schools, and police. If they don't get it from income tax, they get it elsewhere.

### 1. Property Taxes
Texas and New Hampshire have some of the highest property tax rates in the nation. If you own a home, your "tax savings" might just shift from your paycheck to your mortgage escrow.

### 2. Sales Tax
Tennessee and Washington have high sales taxes (combining state + local can exceed 9-10%). This is a "regressive" tax that hits lower-income earners harder. Use our [Sales Tax Calculator](/sales-tax-calculator) to see the impact on your purchases.

### 3. Cost of Living
Alaska is expensive because everything must be shipped in. Florida and Nevada (Vegas) have high insurance and housing costs.

## Who Benefits Most?

*   **High Earners:** Since income tax is progressive, cutting it out saves high earners the most.
*   **Renters:** You avoid the high property taxes (though rent may reflect it).
*   **Remote Workers:** If you can keep your California salary but live in Nevada, you win.

## Summary

Moving to a no-tax state is a powerful way to increase wealth, but do the math on the *total* cost of living, not just the income tax.
