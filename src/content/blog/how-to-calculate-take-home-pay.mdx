## The Paycheck Shock

You signed an offer letter for $5,000 a month. You get your first check, and it is for $3,800. Panic sets in. Did they make a mistake?

Likely not. You just met **Net Pay**.

## The Formula

`Net Pay = Gross Pay - (PreTax Deductions + Taxes + PostTax Deductions)`

## Step 1: Gross Pay
This is the "sticker price" of your labor.
*   **Salary:** Annual / Pay Periods.
*   **Hourly:** Rate x Hours Worked.

## Step 2: Pre-Tax Deductions (The Good Stuff)
These come out *before* taxes, lowering your tax bill.
*   **401(k):** Retirement savings.
*   **Health Insurance:** Premiums (usually).
*   **HSA/FSA:** Medical savings.

## Step 3: Taxes (The Inevitable)
*   **Federal Income Tax:** Based on your W-4 settings and tax bracket.
*   **State/Local Tax:** Depends on where you live/work.
*   **FICA:** A flat 7.65% for Social Security and Medicare.

## Step 4: Post-Tax Deductions
Money taken out *after* taxes.
*   **Roth 401(k):** Retirement savings taxed now for tax-free growth later.
*   **Garnishments:** Child support, unpaid debts.
*   **Life Insurance:** Supplemental plans.

## Example: $60,000 Salary (Biweekly)

*   **Gross:** $2,307
*   **401k (5%):** -$115 (Reduces taxable income)
*   **Health Ins:** -$100
*   **Federal Tax:** -$250 (Estimate)
*   **State Tax:** -$100 (Estimate)
*   **FICA:** -$176
*   **Net Pay:** **$1,566**

## Summary

Your "real" income is your Net Pay. Always build your household budget based on this number, not your Gross Pay.
