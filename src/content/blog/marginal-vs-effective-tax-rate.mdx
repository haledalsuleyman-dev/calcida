If you’ve ever heard “I’m in the 24% bracket,” it’s easy to assume you pay 24% on all your income. That’s not how progressive taxes work.

Two definitions:

- **Marginal tax rate**: the tax rate on your next dollar of taxable income
- **Effective tax rate**: total tax ÷ total income

Estimate your effective rate quickly with the [Effective Tax Rate Calculator](/effective-tax-rate-calculator).

## Why marginal and effective rates differ

In progressive systems, income is taxed in layers. Lower layers are taxed at lower rates, so your average (effective) rate is usually lower than your marginal rate.

## Example (simplified)

If your total income is $100,000 and total tax is $18,000:

Effective rate = $18,000 / $100,000 = 18%

But your marginal rate might be 22% or 24% depending on brackets. That higher marginal rate applies only to the “top slice” of your taxable income.

## Why this matters for real decisions

- **Raises and bonuses**: marginal rate affects incremental take-home, but effective rate reflects your overall picture.
- **Budgeting**: plan from net income, not gross.
- **Tax planning**: deductions and credits can reduce taxable income and total tax.

## FAQ

### Is effective tax rate the same as average tax rate?
Yes, in common usage. Effective tax rate is the average tax rate on your total income.

### Does my marginal rate determine how much of my paycheck I lose?
It influences the tax on incremental income, but withholding is an estimate and can differ per paycheck.

### Can my effective rate be higher than my marginal rate?
It’s uncommon for federal income tax alone, but total tax burden can be higher when you include payroll taxes and state/local taxes.

### Do deductions affect marginal rate?
Deductions reduce taxable income, which can reduce the amount taxed at higher brackets.
