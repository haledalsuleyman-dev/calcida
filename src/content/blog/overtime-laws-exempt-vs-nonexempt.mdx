Overtime rules can be confusing because eligibility is based on **classification**, not just whether you’re paid hourly or salary.

At a high level:

- **Non-exempt** workers are typically eligible for overtime pay.
- **Exempt** workers are typically not eligible for overtime pay.

Classification depends on role duties and other factors, so when in doubt, confirm with HR and your local labor rules.

If you’re eligible and want to estimate overtime pay, use the [Overtime Pay Calculator](/overtime-calculator).

## Salary does not automatically mean “no overtime”

Many salaried roles are exempt, but some salaried roles can still be non-exempt. The key is the classification and job duties tests, not the pay frequency.

## FAQ

### Why does exempt vs non-exempt matter?
It determines whether you’re eligible for overtime pay and how your hours are treated for payroll.

### If I’m salaried, can I still get overtime?
Sometimes. It depends on classification and job duties. Some employers pay additional compensation even when not required.

### What if I work “off the clock”?
That can be a compliance issue. Track your hours and speak with HR. Rules vary by jurisdiction.

### How do I estimate my effective hourly rate?
