Property taxes are one of the biggest “non-mortgage” costs of homeownership. Even if you have a great interest rate, higher property taxes can push your **total monthly housing cost** higher than expected.

To estimate your annual and monthly property taxes, use the [Property Tax Calculator](/property-tax-calculator).

## How property taxes are calculated (simplified)

Most estimates follow the same core structure:

1. Start with a value (market value or assessed value)
2. Subtract exemptions (if any)
3. Multiply by a local tax rate

Example:

- Home value: $450,000
- Property tax rate: 1.10%
- Exemption: $0

Estimated annual tax ≈ $450,000 × 1.10% = $4,950

## Assessed value vs market value

Some counties tax a property based on an **assessed value**, which may differ from market value. In those cases:

- use assessed value if you can find it, or
- use market value as a planning estimate

## Exemptions (homestead and other reductions)

Many jurisdictions offer exemptions that reduce taxable value, such as:

- homestead exemption (primary residence)
- senior/disabled exemptions
- veteran exemptions

If you have an exemption amount, include it in the [Property Tax Calculator](/property-tax-calculator) to estimate the reduced bill.

## How taxes affect your mortgage payment (escrow)

If your loan uses escrow, your lender collects property taxes monthly:

Monthly escrow estimate ≈ (Annual property taxes) / 12

That’s why two homes with the same price and interest rate can have very different total payments. Model the full monthly cost with the [Mortgage Payment Calculator](/mortgage-payment-calculator).

## FAQ

### Why did my property taxes go up?
Taxes can rise due to reassessment, local budget changes, new construction/renovations, or changes in the tax rate.

### Do lenders always require escrow?
Not always. Some loans require escrow, while others allow you to pay taxes directly. Escrow rules vary by loan type and down payment.

### Are property taxes included in APR?
No. Property taxes are not typically included in mortgage APR. APR focuses on finance charges (interest plus certain fees).

### How accurate is an online property tax estimate?
It’s a planning estimate. Exact taxes depend on assessed value and local rules. The goal is to prevent surprises in monthly housing cost.

### How do property taxes affect affordability?
Higher taxes raise the monthly payment, reducing the home price you can comfortably afford. Use the [Mortgage Affordability Calculator](/mortgage-affordability-calculator) and include taxes.
