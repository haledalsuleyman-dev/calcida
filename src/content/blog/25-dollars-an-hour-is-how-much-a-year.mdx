## The Quick Answer

If you work **40 hours a week** for **52 weeks a year**:

**$25/hour = $52,000 per year.**

### The Math
`$25 (rate) x 40 (hours) x 52 (weeks) = $52,000`

## Breakdown by Pay Period

*   **Annual:** $52,000
*   **Monthly:** $4,333
*   **Biweekly (every 2 weeks):** $2,000
*   **Weekly:** $1,000
*   **Daily (8 hours):** $200

## The "Real World" Factors

While $52,000 is the base number, real life changes the math.

### 1. Unpaid Time Off
If you don't get paid vacation days (common for hourly contractors), taking 2 weeks off reduces your income.
*   **50 weeks x 40 hours x $25 = $50,000.**
*   *Cost of vacation:* $2,000.

### 2. Overtime
If you work 5 hours of overtime per week at "time and a half" ($37.50/hr):
*   **Regular:** $52,000
*   **Overtime:** 5 hrs x 52 weeks x $37.50 = $9,750
*   **Total:** **$61,750**

Overtime can significantly boost an hourly wage compared to a fixed salary. Use our [Overtime Pay Calculator](/overtime-calculator) to see your earning potential.

## What About Taxes?

$52,000 is your **Gross Income**. Your **Net Income** (take-home pay) will be lower.

Estimated Taxes (Single Filer, Standard Deduction):
*   **Federal Tax:** ~$4,300
*   **FICA (SS + Medicare):** ~$3,978
*   **State Tax:** Varies (0% to 6%+)

**Estimated Take-Home:** ~$43,700 (approx. $3,640/month). For a precise estimate, use the [Paycheck Calculator](/paycheck-calculator).

## Is $25/Hour Good?

*   **National Average:** It is near the median individual income in the US.
*   **Living Wage:** In many mid-sized cities, $25/hr allows for a comfortable, modest lifestyle. In high-cost hubs like NYC or SF, budget will be tight.

## Summary

Knowing your annual equivalent helps you compare hourly jobs against salaried offers. $25/hr is a solid baseline of $52k, but overtime can push it much higher.
